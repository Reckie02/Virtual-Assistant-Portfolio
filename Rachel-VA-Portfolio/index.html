Case Study

Deep dive: Managed Email System

A real-world example of how I transformed a chaotic inbox into a streamlined communication engine.

📧

Executive Inbox Management for a Remote Founder

Client: US-based startup founder · Timeframe: Ongoing retainer · Tools: Gmail, Google Calendar, Notion, Slack

The Challenge

The client, a solo founder running a growing SaaS startup, was spending 3–4 hours daily on email. Their inbox held 1,400+ unread messages, with critical client requests buried under newsletters and CC chains. They were missing follow-ups and losing deals.

My Approach

1

Inbox Audit: Spent the first two days categorising every email — client, vendor, newsletter, internal, urgent, and archive. Tagged by priority and project.

2

System Setup: Created a colour-coded label system in Gmail (🔴 Urgent / 🟡 Action Needed / 🟢 FYI / ⚫ Archive). Set up filters to auto-label and auto-archive newsletters and receipts.

3

Canned Responses: Drafted 12 templated responses for the most frequent email types — meeting requests, partnership enquiries, customer support, and invoice follow-ups.

4

Daily Briefing: Each morning I sent a Slack message summarising the top 3–5 emails requiring the founder's attention, with a draft reply ready for their approval.

5

Follow-up Tracker: Built a Notion board to track all outbound emails awaiting reply, with automated reminders at 48 hours and 5 days.

The Results

3 hrs
Saved daily for the founder
98%
Inbox cleared within 48 hrs
60%
Faster average response time

Key Takeaway

The biggest win wasn't just a cleaner inbox — it was giving the founder back their mental space. By knowing that nothing would fall through the cracks, they could focus entirely on product and growth. This is what good VA work really looks like: invisible infrastructure that makes everything run smoothly.
